What is an Affidavit?

An affidavit is a written statement of facts made voluntarily by a person under oath. The person making the statement (called the deponent) declares that the information written in the affidavit is true.

To ensure legal authenticity, the affidavit must usually be:

  • Signed by the person making the statement
  • Verified before a Notary Public
  • Stamped and sealed with a notary stamp

A notarized affidavit is widely accepted by courts, government departments, banks, and other institutions.
