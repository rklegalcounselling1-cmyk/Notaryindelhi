What is a Declaration Document?

A declaration is a formal written statement made by an individual confirming that the information provided is true and correct to the best of their knowledge.

A declaration may be required for various legal, administrative, or official purposes. To make the declaration legally valid and acceptable by authorities, it is often signed before a Notary Public and notarized.

A notarized declaration helps ensure that:

  • The person making the declaration is properly identified
  • The statement is voluntarily made
  • The document carries legal authenticity

Why Declaration Notarization is Required

Notarization of a declaration is important because it confirms the authenticity of the document and the identity of the person signing it.

Key benefits of notarized declaration documents include:

  • ✔ Legal authenticity and verification
  • ✔ Acceptance by courts and government offices
  • ✔ Prevention of fraud or false declarations
  • ✔ Proper identification of the declarant
  • ✔ Increased legal credibility of the document

Many government departments and organizations require declarations to be signed before a notary public to be accepted as valid documentation.
