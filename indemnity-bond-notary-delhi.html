What is an Indemnity Bond?

An Indemnity Bond is a legal agreement in which one party promises to compensate another party for any loss, damage, or liability that may arise in the future.

The person giving the bond (indemnifier) agrees to protect the other party (indemnified) from potential losses or claims.

Indemnity bonds are widely used in:

  • Government departments
  • Banks and financial institutions
  • Property transactions
  • Lost document declarations
  • Educational institutions
  • Court procedures

For legal validity, the indemnity bond is usually prepared on stamp paper and notarized by a Notary Public.
